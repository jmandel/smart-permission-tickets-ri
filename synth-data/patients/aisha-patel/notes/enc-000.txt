MEDICAL DECISION MAKING:
This child presented with anaphylaxis involving three organ systems (cutaneous, respiratory, cardiovascular) following accidental peanut ingestion. The clinical picture was unambiguous and met consensus diagnostic criteria for anaphylaxis. Epinephrine was administered promptly as the first-line treatment. Adjunctive therapy with antihistamine, corticosteroid, and bronchodilator was provided per standard of care. Elevated serum tryptase at 18.4 ng/mL provides biochemical confirmation of the anaphylactic mechanism. The patient was observed for 6 hours given the risk of biphasic reaction, which can occur in up to 20% of pediatric anaphylaxis cases. No biphasic reaction was observed. Given this is the patients first known allergic reaction and first known peanut exposure, referral to a pediatric allergist is essential for comprehensive evaluation including specific IgE testing and development of a long-term management plan.
