MEDICAL DECISION-MAKING:
This 4-year-old female with known moderate persistent asthma on dual-controller therapy presented with an acute severe exacerbation (J45.51) triggered by a viral upper respiratory infection. Initial SpO2 of 89% on room air, tachypnea to 38, and significant accessory muscle use were consistent with severe exacerbation. She was treated with an aggressive bronchodilator protocol (continuous nebulized albuterol x3, ipratropium x2) and systemic corticosteroids (IV methylprednisolone 1 mg/kg). She demonstrated progressive and sustained improvement over a 4-hour observation period with SpO2 stabilizing at 96% on room air, normalization of respiratory rate, and resolution of retractions. Given her adequate response to treatment and stable room air saturations for over 2 hours, she is appropriate for discharge rather than admission. Notably, this degree of exacerbation on dual-controller therapy (fluticasone/salmeterol 45/21 plus montelukast) suggests her asthma may warrant reclassification and therapy escalation, which should be addressed by her pediatrician and allergist.
