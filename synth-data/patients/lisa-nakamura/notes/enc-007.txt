SUBJECTIVE:
Lisa Nakamura is a 36-year-old G3P0030 woman who presents for her scheduled 8-week follow-up ultrasound in her third pregnancy. She reports that her nausea and breast tenderness have diminished over the past week, which has raised her concern. She has been compliant with vaginal micronized progesterone 200 mg nightly as prescribed at her 6-week visit. She denies vaginal bleeding, cramping, fever, or other acute complaints. She and her husband Ryan continue to be hopeful but understandably anxious given their history of two prior first-trimester losses.

Pertinent OB history: G3P0030. First loss at 6 weeks (June 2024, spontaneous abortion, expectant management). Second loss at 7 weeks (November 2024, missed abortion, expectant management). RPL workup performed December 2024: parental karyotypes normal (Lisa 46,XX; Ryan 46,XY), TSH 2.1 mIU/L, HbA1c 5.2%, coagulation panel normal, ANA negative. Third pregnancy confirmed January 2025 with encouraging 6-week ultrasound showing fetal cardiac activity at 112 bpm.

Vitals: BP 118/74 mmHg, HR 76 bpm, Temp 98.4 F, RR 16, SpO2 99%, Weight 130 lbs, BMI 21.6.

General: Well-appearing woman in no acute distress. Appears anxious.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Missed abortion at 8 weeks gestation — third consecutive first-trimester pregnancy loss. Fetal pole growth arrest at approximately 7w1d with absent cardiac activity.
2. Recurrent pregnancy loss (RPL) — now meeting classic criteria of three or more consecutive first-trimester losses. Prior RPL workup (karyotypes, thyroid, HbA1c, coagulation, ANA) unrevealing.
3. History of vaginal progesterone supplementation this pregnancy — to be discontinued.

PLAN:
1. Dilation and curettage (D&C) recommended rather than expectant management for two reasons: (a) to expedite physical recovery given the emotional toll of three consecutive losses, and (b) to obtain products of conception (POC) for cytogenetic analysis, which may provide critical diagnostic information regarding the etiology of her recurrent losses.
2. D&C scheduled for 03/12/2025 at Rocky Mountain Women's Health affiliated ambulatory surgery center. Risks, benefits, and alternatives discussed at length. Patient understands and consents.
3. POC to be sent for histopathology and cytogenetic karyotype analysis.
4. Discontinue vaginal progesterone 200 mg nightly.
5. Follow-up beta-hCG to be drawn approximately 2 weeks post-procedure to confirm complete resolution.
6. Emotional support provided. Patient tearful but composed. Encouraged continuation of therapy through EAP. Husband Ryan present and supportive.

ADDENDUM — 03/12/2025:
D&C performed at affiliated ambulatory surgery center. Procedure uncomplicated. Products of conception obtained and sent to pathology for histopathology and to cytogenetics laboratory for karyotype analysis.

ADDENDUM — Results (received approximately 04/01/2025):
Histopathology: Confirmed products of conception with chorionic villi identified. No molar changes.
Cytogenetic analysis of POC: Normal female karyotype (46,XX).

This is a pivotal finding. A chromosomally normal (euploid) conceptus eliminates embryonic aneuploidy — the most common cause of sporadic early miscarriage — as the explanation for this loss. Combined with the prior normal RPL workup (normal parental karyotypes, normal thyroid, normal HbA1c, normal coagulation, negative ANA), this shifts clinical suspicion toward a maternal factor, particularly antiphospholipid syndrome (APS).

Follow-up beta-hCG on 03/24/2025: <5 mIU/mL, confirming complete resolution.

Updated problem list: Recurrent pregnancy loss — three consecutive first-trimester losses, one with confirmed euploid conceptus.

Next steps: APS antibody panel to be ordered at next visit. Plan to evaluate anticardiolipin antibodies (IgG and IgM), lupus anticoagulant, and anti-beta-2 glycoprotein I antibodies. If positive, confirmatory testing at 12 weeks per revised Sapporo criteria will be required. Specialist referrals to MFM and hematology anticipated pending results.
