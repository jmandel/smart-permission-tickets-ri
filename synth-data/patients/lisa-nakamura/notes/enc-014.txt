Meets revised Sapporo (Sydney) classification criteria based on clinical criterion (three consecutive unexplained early pregnancy losses before 10 weeks gestation) in combination with laboratory criteria (persistently positive anticardiolipin IgG at moderate titer, persistently positive lupus anticoagulant, and now-positive anti-beta-2 glycoprotein I IgG confirmed at greater than 12 weeks from initial testing). PLAN:
Anticoagulation protocol for future pregnancy discussed in detail with patient and husband:

1. PRECONCEPTION: Start low-dose aspirin 81 mg PO daily. Coordinated with Dr. Whitfield (MFM). To begin when patient is ready to attempt conception.

2. UPON POSITIVE PREGNANCY TEST: Initiate enoxaparin 40 mg subcutaneous once daily (prophylactic dose).

3. DURING PREGNANCY: Monthly anti-Xa level monitoring (target peak 0.2-0.6 IU/mL). Dose adjustment if significant weight change. Transition to unfractionated heparin at 36 weeks gestation in preparation for delivery.

4. PERIPARTUM: Hold enoxaparin 24 hours prior to planned delivery. Resume enoxaparin 6-12 hours postpartum (vaginal delivery) or 12-24 hours postpartum (cesarean delivery).

5. POSTPARTUM: Continue enoxaparin or transition to warfarin for 6 weeks postpartum. Warfarin is compatible with breastfeeding if patient prefers oral anticoagulation.

6. LONG-TERM: No indication for lifelong anticoagulation in absence of thrombotic events. Permanent avoidance of combined estrogen-containing contraceptives. Thromboprophylaxis recommended for future high-risk situations (surgery, prolonged immobilization).

Counseling provided: With standard treatment (aspirin plus prophylactic heparin), live birth rate in obstetric APS improves from approximately 10% untreated to approximately 70% with therapy.
