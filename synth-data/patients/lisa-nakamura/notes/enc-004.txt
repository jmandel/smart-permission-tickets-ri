ASSESSMENT:
1. Missed abortion (embryonic demise) at 7 weeks gestation — second consecutive first-trimester pregnancy loss. Initial beta-hCG kinetics were reassuring and 6-week ultrasound showed a gestational sac with yolk sac, but fetal pole was small for dates without cardiac activity. One-week follow-up ultrasound confirmed no interval growth and absent heartbeat. CRL consistent with growth arrest at approximately 6 weeks 2 days. Patient elected expectant management and passed products of conception spontaneously over 10 days. Beta-hCG confirmed complete resolution on 11/04/2024.

2. Recurrent pregnancy loss — two consecutive first-trimester losses (June 2024 and October 2024). While the classic definition requires three or more losses, current evidence supports initiating workup after two losses, particularly given patient age (35) and significant emotional distress. No prior RPL evaluation has been performed.

3. Mild depressive symptoms — PHQ-2 score of 2, subthreshold. Appropriate grief reaction in context of recurrent pregnancy loss. Will monitor; formal screening at follow-up.
