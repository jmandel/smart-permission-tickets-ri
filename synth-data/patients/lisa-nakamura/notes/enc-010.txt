ASSESSMENT:
1. Recurrent pregnancy loss (G3P0030) — three consecutive first-trimester losses, most recent with chromosomally normal conceptus (46,XX), indicating likely maternal etiology.
2. Suspected antiphospholipid syndrome, obstetric type — elevated anticardiolipin IgG (48 GPL) and weakly positive lupus anticoagulant meet laboratory criteria pending confirmatory testing at 12 weeks per revised Sapporo/Sydney criteria. Anti-beta-2 glycoprotein I IgG borderline. Awaiting confirmatory repeat testing in July 2025.
3. Otherwise healthy 36-year-old woman with no other medical comorbidities.

Clinical reasoning: The combination of three consecutive early losses, a chromosomally normal third loss, negative standard RPL workup, and positive antiphospholipid antibodies is highly suggestive of obstetric APS as the underlying etiology. Treatment with aspirin plus prophylactic-dose LMWH significantly improves live birth rates in APS-associated recurrent pregnancy loss. Definitive diagnosis and management plan are contingent on confirmatory antibody testing at 12 weeks.

PLAN:
Provisional high-risk pregnancy management plan, contingent upon confirmatory APS testing and coordination with Dr. James Okafor, Hematology, UC Health:

1. Preconception:
   - Low-dose aspirin 81 mg PO daily, to be initiated once decision to conceive is made (ideally 1 month prior to conception).

2. Upon confirmed pregnancy:
   - Begin enoxaparin 40 mg subcutaneous daily (prophylactic-dose LMWH).

3. First trimester surveillance:
   - Transvaginal ultrasound every 2 weeks to confirm viability and interval growth.

4. Second trimester:
   - Standard anatomy scan at 18-20 weeks gestation.
   - Serial growth ultrasounds beginning at 24 weeks, every 3-4 weeks.

5. Third trimester:
   - Fetal Doppler assessment if growth concerns arise.
   - Non-stress testing beginning at 32 weeks, twice weekly.
   - Close preeclampsia surveillance: serial BP monitoring, urine protein assessment, laboratory evaluation as indicated.

6. Delivery planning:
   - Planned delivery at 37-39 weeks gestation depending on clinical course.
   - Delivery to occur at facility with Level III NICU capability.

7. Postpartum:
   - Continue anticoagulation therapy for 6 weeks postpartum per hematology guidance.

Counseling provided: Discussed with patient and her husband that even with optimal treatment, APS pregnancies carry elevated risks for preeclampsia, fetal growth restriction, and preterm delivery requiring close monitoring throughout gestation. Emphasized that this plan is provisional and will be finalized following confirmatory APS testing results and coordination with hematology.

Care plan documented. Consultation summary to be sent to referring physician Dr. Forsythe.
