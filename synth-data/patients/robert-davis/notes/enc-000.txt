EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT COURSE:
Given the clinical presentation of chronic cough with hemoptysis, constitutional symptoms, and classic CXR findings of a right upper lobe cavitary lesion, concern for pulmonary tuberculosis was immediately high. Airborne isolation precautions were initiated and the patient was placed in a negative-pressure room. Three sputum specimens were ordered for AFB smear and mycobacterial culture; the first specimen was collected in the ED at 15:40 and returned positive for AFB (2+ on ZN stain). Rapid molecular testing with GeneXpert MTB/RIF confirmed M. tuberculosis complex without rifampin resistance. Baseline labs including CBC, CMP, HbA1c, HIV, hepatitis B, and hepatitis C were obtained for pre-treatment assessment. HIV and hepatitis serologies were non-reactive/negative. Mild normocytic anemia and hypoalbuminemia are consistent with chronic illness. Elevated glucose and HbA1c of 8.1% reflect worsening glycemic control in the setting of active infection in a patient with known type 2 diabetes.

MEDICAL DECISION MAKING:
This is a 65-year-old male with a classic presentation for pulmonary tuberculosis: subacute cough with hemoptysis, B symptoms (night sweats, weight loss, anorexia), and CXR demonstrating a cavitary right upper lobe lesion. AFB smear positivity and GeneXpert confirmation of M. tuberculosis without rifampin resistance support initiation of standard four-drug RIPE therapy. The patient requires inpatient admission for treatment initiation under airborne isolation, monitoring of hepatic function given planned hepatotoxic medications in a patient with diabetes, and coordination with public health authorities. His elevated BP (142/88) is likely related to the acute setting; home antihypertensive (lisinopril 20 mg) was continued. His diabetes is poorly controlled (A1c 8.1%) and will require close monitoring given anticipated drug interactions and metabolic stress of active TB.
