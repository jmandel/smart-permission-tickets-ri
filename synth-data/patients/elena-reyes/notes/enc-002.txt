ASSESSMENT

   Newly diagnosed at 28 weeks gestation. All four time points of the 3-hour OGTT are abnormal. The degree of hyperglycemia is mild-to-moderate: fasting glucose only 1 mg/dL above threshold (96 vs. ≥95), with the most prominent elevation at 1 hour post-challenge (198 mg/dL). A1c of 5.4% indicates recent glucose elevation consistent with gestational context rather than pre-existing diabetes. No prior history of glucose abnormality. Given the fasting value and the overall pattern, initial management with medical nutrition therapy and lifestyle modification is appropriate; insulin therapy is not indicated at this time.

PLAN: MEDICAL NUTRITION THERAPY

Carbohydrate Counting Education:
— Provided instruction on identifying carbohydrate sources (grains, starchy vegetables, fruits, milk/yogurt)
— Target carbohydrate distribution:
     ° Breakfast: ~45g carbohydrate (most insulin-resistant meal in pregnancy)
     ° Lunch: ~60g carbohydrate
     ° Dinner: ~60g carbohydrate
     ° Snack 1 (mid-morning): 15–30g carbohydrate
     ° Snack 2 (mid-afternoon): 15–30g carbohydrate
     ° Snack 3 (evening, if needed): 15–30g carbohydrate
— Each meal should include protein and healthy fat to slow carbohydrate absorption

Specific Recommendations:
— Breakfast examples: Oatmeal (1/2 cup dry = 27g carb) with berries (1/2 cup = 10g) + egg whites; whole-grain toast (1 slice = 12–15g) with peanut butter + fruit
— Lunch/Dinner: Lean protein (chicken, fish, or lean beef 3–4 oz), non-starchy vegetables (unlimited), and controlled carbohydrate (1/3 cup rice or pasta = 15g, or small baked potato = 15–20g)
— Snacks: Cheese and small apple; Greek yogurt; almonds; whole-grain crackers with hummus
— Avoid: Sugary beverages, juices, candy, pastries, and high-carbohydrate prepared foods

Lifestyle:
— Walking: Encourage a 15-minute walk after each meal to improve post-meal glucose disposal. This is particularly effective at reducing blood glucose peaks and does not require vigorous activity.
— Sleep and stress: Reinforce the importance of adequate sleep (7–9 hours) and stress management, as both affect insulin sensitivity

Blood Glucose Self-Monitoring:
— Provided glucose meter and lancets
— Fasting measurement: Upon waking, before eating
— 1-hour post-meal: Exactly 1 hour after the start of the first bite of the meal
— Frequency: Every meal × 1-hour post-meal for the first week to establish a pattern; may reduce frequency if values are consistently in target range after review with OB provider
— Target values:
     ° Fasting: <95 mg/dL
     ° 1-hour post-meal: <140 mg/dL
— Patient instructed on meter operation, lancet technique, and proper sample collection
— Provided blood glucose log to record all values and times

Patient Education:
— Explained that gestational diabetes occurs in approximately 2–10% of pregnancies and is caused by pregnancy hormones reducing insulin sensitivity
— Clarified that GDM is not due to dietary excess and is not preventable, but is very manageable with nutrition and lifestyle
— Emphasized that good glucose control reduces risk of fetal macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycemia, and other complications
— Discussed that GDM typically resolves after delivery
— Explained the increased lifetime risk of type 2 diabetes following GDM and the importance of postpartum screening
