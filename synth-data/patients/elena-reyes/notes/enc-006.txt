Risks and benefits of treatment options were discussed in detail.

Outside labs reviewed: positive rheumatoid factor, strongly positive anti-CCP, elevated ESR/CRP.

ASSESSMENT:

2. High-risk medication initiation. Baseline CBC, renal function, liver enzymes, albumin, and hepatitis B screening are acceptable for methotrexate start.

3. Reviewed methotrexate risks/benefits and monitoring requirements, including hepatotoxicity, cytopenias, GI side effects, infection precautions, and need for CBC/CMP monitoring every 8-12 weeks.
4. Counseled regarding alcohol limitation.
5. Reviewed teratogenicity and discussed need for reliable contraception while taking methotrexate.
6. Baseline labs reviewed as above; continue routine safety monitoring.
7. Follow up in approximately 8 weeks for reassessment of symptoms, joint counts, and medication tolerance.
