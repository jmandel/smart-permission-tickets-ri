ASSESSMENT & MEDICAL DECISION-MAKING

36-year-old female with acute right ankle inversion injury sustained 3 hours ago. Clinical examination is consistent with lateral ligament sprain, specifically anterior talofibular ligament (ATFL) involvement. Anterior drawer test shows increased laxity; inversion stress test positive. Tenderness maximal over ATFL region. X-rays negative for fracture or dislocation. Ottawa Ankle Rules criteria negative, but radiographs obtained given mechanism and clinical uncertainty.

Diagnosis: Grade II lateral ankle sprain, right ankle (ICD-10: S93.401A).
Rationale: Grade II characterized by partial tearing of ligament(s), evident by positive anterior drawer and inversion stress findings, moderate soft tissue swelling, and functional instability without complete ligament failure. Patient maintains neurovascular integrity and is able to ambulate with pain.

Special considerations:
1. Patient is on adalimumab (biologic DMARD) for seropositive RA and methotrexate. Biologic therapy may slightly increase infection risk and potentially affect wound healing, though acute injury with intact skin and negative X-rays does not suggest complicated course.
2. Recent NSAID use (self-administered ibuprofen 400 mg 2 hours prior). Short-course NSAID is acceptable for acute musculoskeletal injury; recommended to patient's rheumatologist given methotrexate co-therapy.
3. No signs of neurovascular compromise or compartment syndrome.
