SUBJECTIVE:
Chief complaint: Right ankle pain after inversion injury.

HPI: Elena Marisol Reyes is a 34-year-old woman who presented to urgent care the evening of 08/17/2024 after inverting her right ankle while walking on a trail with her children. She reports immediate pain over the lateral ankle with swelling and difficulty bearing weight. Denies numbness, tingling, or other injury. No head trauma or loss of consciousness. Medication reconciliation reviewed and notable for methotrexate 15 mg weekly, folic acid 1 mg daily, adalimumab 40 mg every 2 weeks, and loratadine as needed. Allergy: amoxicillin (hives/rash). Relevant PMH includes rheumatoid arthritis on DMARD/biologic therapy.

OBJECTIVE:
Vitals: BP 124/78 mmHg, HR 88 bpm, Temp 98.6 F, RR 16, SpO2 98%, Wt 150 lb

General: Alert, in no acute distress.
MSK: Right ankle with lateral malleolar soft tissue swelling and tenderness most pronounced over the anterior talofibular ligament distribution. Pain with weight bearing. No gross deformity. No gross instability on exam to suggest complete ligament rupture. Distal pulses intact. Sensation intact distally. No focal tenderness suggesting proximal fibula injury.
Neurovascular: Foot warm and well perfused; cap refill normal; sensation preserved.
Skin: No open wound or ecchymosis noted beyond lateral ankle swelling.

Imaging: Right ankle X-ray, 3 views, reviewed today. No acute fracture or dislocation. Mild lateral soft tissue swelling only.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Grade II right lateral ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament, acute.
2. Rheumatoid arthritis on methotrexate and adalimumab, relevant to medication counseling.

PLAN:
1. Air stirrup brace provided for support/stabilization.
2. Recommend RICE measures: rest, ice, compression/support, and elevation.
3. Ibuprofen 400 mg by mouth every 8 hours as needed for pain for up to 5 days. Discussed short-course use only given concurrent rheumatoid arthritis medications.
4. Progressive weight bearing as tolerated.
5. Reviewed X-ray results with patient; no fracture seen, so conservative management appropriate at this time.
6. Return precautions discussed: worsening pain, increasing swelling, numbness, inability to walk, new instability, or other concerning symptoms.
7. Follow up with PCP/orthopedics if not improving as expected over the next 1-2 weeks or sooner for worsening symptoms.

Patient verbalized understanding of diagnosis, imaging findings, home care instructions, and follow-up plan.
