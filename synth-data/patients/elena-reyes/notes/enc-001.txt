ASSESSMENT:

Missed Abortion (Intrauterine Fetal Demise), First Trimester, at approximately 9 weeks 4 days menstrual age. ICD-10-CM: O02.1.

Clinical Impression: Patient is hemodynamically stable with no active vaginal bleeding. Vital signs normal. Laboratory values reassuring (normal hemoglobin, no leukocytosis). Cervix closed. Pelvic examination reveals no acute pathology. Patient is an appropriate candidate for expectant management.

PLAN:

1. Management Option Discussion:
All three management options for first-trimester pregnancy loss were reviewed with patient in detail:
  a) Expectant management (spontaneous passage of tissue at home over 1-2 weeks)
  b) Medical management (misoprostol administration to induce uterine contractions and passage)
  c) Surgical management (uterine evacuation/dilation and curettage under anesthesia)

Elena elected expectant management after thorough counseling and discussion of risks/benefits of each approach.

2. Expectant Management Counseling:
Detailed verbal and written counseling provided regarding:
  - Expected timeline: tissue passage typically occurs within 1-2 weeks of diagnosis
  - Activity: pelvic rest recommended; avoid intercourse, tampons, douching
  - Warning signs requiring immediate emergency department evaluation:
    * Vaginal bleeding soaking more than one pad per hour
    * Fever greater than 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit
    * Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
    * No passage of tissue within 2 weeks
    * Lightheadedness, syncope, or signs of shock
  - Over-the-counter analgesia (ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours as needed for cramping) acceptable
  - Expect passage of tissue, blood clots, and tissue fragments

3. Emotional Support and Counseling:
Extended counseling session provided (>25 minutes). Patient expressed sadness and guilt regarding the loss. Reassurance provided that first-trimester pregnancy losses are most commonly due to chromosomal abnormalities incompatible with life, and that nothing Elena did or failed to do caused this loss. Emphasized that this is not her fault and is a common occurrence (approximately 1 in 4 clinically recognized pregnancies). Validated emotional response. Information provided on perinatal loss support resources and groups available locally. Partner Marco encouraged to attend follow-up visit for continued support and counseling.

4. Monitoring and Follow-up:
  a) Repeat quantitative beta-hCG ordered for February 8, 2020 (48 hours) to confirm downward trend, confirming loss of pregnancy viability
  b) Telephone follow-up scheduled for February 8, 2020 to review repeat beta-hCG results and confirm patient remains asymptomatic
  c) In-office follow-up appointment scheduled for approximately February 20, 2020 (2 weeks post-diagnosis) for pelvic examination and transvaginal ultrasound to confirm complete passage of products of conception

5. Patient Education:
Written instructions on expectant management of first-trimester pregnancy loss provided. Reviewed warning signs verbally and in writing.
