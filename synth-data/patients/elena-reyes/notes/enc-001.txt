SUBJECTIVE:
Elena Reyes presents for in-office follow-up with Dr. Carmen Villanueva after first-trimester ultrasound demonstrated pregnancy loss. She reports light vaginal spotting only. Denies heavy bleeding, severe abdominal/pelvic pain, fever, chills, dizziness, or syncope. Emotional distress related to pregnancy loss was acknowledged and support was provided. Management options for missed abortion, including expectant, medical, and procedural management, were reviewed in detail. Patient elects expectant management at this time. Relevant OB history includes prior term vaginal delivery in November 2019.

OBJECTIVE:
Vitals: BP 108/70 mmHg, HR 74 bpm, Temp 98.4 F, RR 14, SpO2 99%, Wt 138 lb

General: Alert, tearful but appropriate, no acute distress.
Abdomen: No significant tenderness reported; no signs of acute abdomen.
Pelvic/OB: Light spotting by history; no report of heavy bleeding.
Ultrasound: Transvaginal ultrasound shows intrauterine pregnancy with crown-rump length consistent with 9 weeks 4 days and no fetal cardiac activity, confirming missed abortion. No adnexal mass or free fluid.
Labs: CBC within normal limits/stable: hemoglobin 12.6 g/dL, WBC 7.8 K/uL, platelets 248 K/uL. Blood type A positive. Rh immune globulin not indicated.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Missed abortion / spontaneous abortion, first trimester, clinically stable. Ultrasound confirms intrauterine pregnancy measuring 9 weeks 4 days without fetal cardiac activity. Patient has only light spotting and no evidence of hemodynamic instability, hemorrhage, ectopic process, or infection.
2. Emotional distress/grief reaction related to pregnancy loss. Counseling and support provided during visit.

PLAN:
1. Proceed with expectant management per patient preference after counseling on all management options.
2. Reviewed warning signs and strict return precautions: soaking more than 2 pads per hour, severe pain, fever, foul discharge, dizziness, or syncope.
3. Rhogam not indicated given A positive blood type.
4. Provided written patient instructions regarding expected bleeding/cramping course and when to seek urgent evaluation.
5. Follow up in 1-2 weeks for reassessment and to confirm completion, sooner for any worsening symptoms or concerns.
6. Emotional support offered; patient encouraged to reach out for additional support as needed.
