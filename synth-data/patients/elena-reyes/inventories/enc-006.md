- **Prescriber:** James Tran, MD, NPI 1673910284
- **Indication/Reason:** Newly diagnosed seropositive rheumatoid arthritis; first-line disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD); early aggressive treatment to prevent irreversible joint damage
- **Expected Onset of Benefit:** 4–8 weeks
- **Counseling/Warnings Documented:**
  * **Teratogenicity:** Category X; absolute contraindication in pregnancy. Patient must use reliable contraception. If pregnancy desired, discontinue methotrexate ≥3 months before conception.
  * **Hepatotoxicity:** Risk of liver fibrosis with chronic use. Mitigation: No alcohol consumption while on methotrexate. LFT monitoring (AST, ALT, albumin, bilirubin) every 8–12 weeks. Discontinue if ALT persistently elevated (>3× ULN).
  * **Myelosuppression:** Risk of WBC, RBC, or platelet reduction. Mitigation: CBC monitoring every 8–12 weeks. Patient instructed to report fever, unusual bruising/bleeding, or severe fatigue.
  * **Nausea:** Most common side effect (20–30% of patients); occurs on day of dose or day after. Folic acid supplementation significantly reduces incidence.
  * **Mucositis, fatigue, rash:** Less common; patient instructed to report.
  * **Monitoring Plan:** CBC and CMP every 8–12 weeks; clinical reassessment at 12 weeks
- **Dose Escalation Plan:** May increase to 20 mg weekly at 8–12 week assessment if inadequate response and well tolerated
- **Do-Not-Use Conditions:** No alcohol. Avoid pregnancy. Not for use in renal impairment (eGFR <30; use caution if 30–60).
- **Interaction Potential:** Note for future coordination with NSAIDs, biologic agents, and renal/hepatic monitoring
