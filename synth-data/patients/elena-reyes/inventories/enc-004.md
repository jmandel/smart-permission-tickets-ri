## Notes on Clinical Significance and FHIR Modeling Decisions

1. **Vital Signs:** All eight vital sign observations represent independent FHIR Observation resources, each with its own LOINC code, reference range, and interpretation. Blood pressure is documented as a component observation (systolic and diastolic components) or as a single BP panel observation with components.

2. **Laboratory Results:** Nine lab values are each separate Observation resources, grouped under a single DiagnosticReport. The report's effective date is the specimen collection date (2022-11-14), distinct from the encounter date (2022-11-08).

3. **Screening:** PHQ-2 can be modeled as a single aggregated Observation with a total score (1/6) or as separate component Observations for Q1 and Q2 answers. Both approaches are valid; the clinical system's design determines the structure.

4. **Conditions — Sensitivity:** The gestational diabetes history (resolved) and pregnancy loss are documented in the problem list per the patient's request to establish comprehensive care coordination in a new system. The clinical note explicitly notes that detailed OB specifics are retained in Texas records and not re-duplicated here "per patient preference." In a real multi-organizational FHIR exchange, a permission ticket could exclude these Condition resources if the patient declines to share OB history with a non-OB provider.

5. **Medication Discontinuation:** Prenatal vitamins are noted as "discontinued" at this encounter, not as an active MedicationRequest. FHIR allows either a completed/stopped MedicationRequest or simply omission from the active medication list. Documenting the discontinuation provides continuity of care context.

6. **Immunizations:** Both vaccines are documented with full administration details (route, site, dose, CVX codes). Prior immunization history (primary COVID series 2021, Tdap 2018) is referenced in the assessment plan but not represented as separate resources in this encounter inventory, as those encounters occurred at different sites.

7. **Documents:** Two distinct DocumentReference resources represent the clinical note and the patient-facing after-visit summary. Both are created the same day but serve different audiences and purposes.

8. **External Lab Specimen:** The lab draw occurred on 2022-11-14 at an external commercial laboratory (not Eastbay Primary Care). In a complete FHIR model, this could be represented as a separate "lab visit" Encounter with type "laboratory" or as a ServiceRequest (order from 11-08) with Specimen and result Observations (11-14). For simplicity in this inventory, the ServiceRequest and Observations are listed without creating a second Encounter.

9. **No Procedures in This Encounter:** While future encounters in Elena's record include procedures (delivery, ultrasound, ankle X-ray at urgent care, QuantiFERON-TB at rheumatology), this new patient wellness visit documents physical examination findings but no procedural codes.
