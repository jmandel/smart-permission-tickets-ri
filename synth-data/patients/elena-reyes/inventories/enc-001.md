## Patient Education and Counseling

### 1. Management Options Counseling

- **Topic:** Patient education on management options for missed abortion
- **Options Reviewed:** Expectant management, medical management (medication-induced), and surgical management (curettage)
- **Patient's Choice:** Expectant management (conservative, watch-and-wait approach; pregnancy tissue passes naturally over time without pharmaceutical or surgical intervention)
- **Documentation:** Counseling documented in progress note; decision-making process and patient understanding reflected in assessment and plan

### 2. Warning Signs and Return Precautions

- **Topic:** Education on warning signs that require urgent evaluation or emergency care
- **Specific Warning Signs Reviewed:**
  - Soaking more than 2 pads per hour (indicates heavy bleeding/hemorrhage)
  - Fever (indicates possible infection)
  - Severe abdominal or pelvic pain (indicates possible complication)
  - Syncope or severe dizziness (indicates hemodynamic compromise)
  - Foul-smelling discharge (indicates possible infection)
- **Instruction:** Patient instructed to seek urgent or emergency evaluation if any of these signs occur
- **Documentation:** Counseling documented in progress note

### 3. Expected Clinical Course

- **Topic:** Education on what to expect with expectant management
- **Points Covered:** Expected vaginal bleeding and cramping over the next 1–2 weeks as pregnancy tissue passes naturally; anticipation of heavier bleeding than the light spotting currently present
- **Written Instruction:** Patient given written patient instructions detailing expected course and when to seek care
