## Patient Education / Counseling Documentation

### Counseling on Management Options

Patient counseled on all three management approaches for first-trimester pregnancy loss:
1. **Expectant management** (spontaneous passage at home over 1–2 weeks) — **SELECTED**
2. **Medical management** (misoprostol to induce passage)
3. **Surgical management** (dilation and curettage/uterine evacuation under anesthesia)

Rationale for expectant management: Patient hemodynamically stable, no vaginal bleeding, no hemorrhage signs, cervix closed, no fever. Clinical stability supports expectant approach.

### Counseling on Expectant Management

**Topics covered:**
- Expected timeline: tissue passage typically 1–2 weeks
- Activity restrictions: pelvic rest; avoid intercourse, tampons, douching
- **Warning signs requiring emergency evaluation:**
  - Vaginal bleeding soaking >1 pad/hour
  - Fever >100.4°F
  - Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
  - No tissue passage within 2 weeks
  - Lightheadedness, syncope, or signs of shock
- Pain management: OTC ibuprofen 600 mg q6h as needed for cramping
- What to expect: passage of tissue, blood clots, tissue fragments
- Follow-up plan: 48-hour phone follow-up, in-office follow-up at 2 weeks

### Emotional Support Counseling

**Documentation Note:** Extended counseling session provided (>25 minutes), substantially above routine prenatal visit time allocation.

**Content:**
- Patient expressed sadness and guilt regarding loss
- Reassurance: First-trimester losses most commonly due to chromosomal abnormalities incompatible with life; not caused by anything patient did
- Normalization: Emphasized that ~1 in 4 clinically recognized pregnancies end in miscarriage
- Validation: Acknowledged emotional difficulty of pregnancy loss
- Resources: Information provided on perinatal loss support groups and resources available locally
- Partner involvement: Marco (patient's spouse) encouraged to attend follow-up visit for continued support and counseling

**Significance:** This emotional support is clinically significant and integral to the encounter. The note explicitly documents this as counseling provided, reflecting standard-of-care perinatal loss management.
