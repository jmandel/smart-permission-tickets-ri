PROCEDURE: Endobronchial ultrasound-guided transbronchial needle aspiration (EBUS-TBNA) of mediastinal lymph nodes, stations 4R and 7.

INDICATION: 62-year-old woman with a 4.2 cm spiculated right upper lobe mass identified on CT chest (08/08/2024) with ipsilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy (stations 4R and 7). PET/CT (09/23/2024) demonstrated FDG-avid RUL mass (SUVmax 12.4) and FDG-avid mediastinal nodes (SUVmax 8.1 at station 4R). Brain MRI (09/25/2024) negative for intracranial metastases. Tissue diagnosis required for staging and treatment planning. Suspected lung malignancy.

ALLERGIES: Sulfonamide antibiotics (rash).

MEDICATIONS: Lisinopril 10 mg PO daily, naproxen 250 mg PO PRN (held pre-procedure).

SEDATION: Moderate sedation administered per institutional protocol.

PRE-PROCEDURE ASSESSMENT:
Vitals: BP 144/88 mmHg, HR 86 bpm, Temp 98.4 F (36.9 C), RR 18, SpO2 95% on room air.
The patient was evaluated and deemed appropriate for moderate sedation. Risks including bleeding, infection, pneumothorax, and need for additional procedures were discussed. Informed consent was obtained. Time-out was performed confirming patient identity, procedure, and site.

PROCEDURE DETAILS:
The patient was positioned supine. Moderate sedation was administered and the patient was monitored continuously. The flexible bronchoscope with integrated ultrasound transducer was introduced via the oral route. Airway inspection was performed; no endobronchial lesions were identified. The mediastinal lymph node stations were systematically evaluated under real-time ultrasound guidance.

Station 4R (right lower paratracheal): Enlarged lymph node identified, measuring approximately 2.1 cm. Three passes were performed with a 22-gauge aspiration needle under direct EBUS visualization. Rapid on-site cytology evaluation (ROSE) confirmed adequate cellular material with atypical cells suspicious for malignancy.

Station 7 (subcarinal): Enlarged lymph node identified. Three passes were performed using the same technique. ROSE confirmed adequate tissue with atypical cells present.

Throughout the procedure, SpO2 was maintained above 93% on 2L supplemental oxygen via nasal cannula. No significant bleeding was encountered. The procedure was completed without complication.

Estimated blood loss: Minimal.
Specimens: Sent to pathology (cytology and cell block) from stations 4R and 7.

POST-PROCEDURE COURSE:
The patient was transferred to the procedural recovery area in stable condition. Post-procedure vital signs were stable. SpO2 returned to 96% on room air. A post-procedure portable chest X-ray was obtained and demonstrated no pneumothorax, no new effusion, and stable appearance of the known RUL mass. The patient tolerated the procedure well, was alert and oriented, and was discharged home in the company of her husband, Emeka Okafor, with standard post-procedure instructions.

COMPLICATIONS: None.

DISPOSITION: Discharged home with accompanying family member.
