Referred for staging evaluation. Never-smoker with significant secondhand smoke exposure. PMH significant for essential hypertension and prediabetes. ECOG performance status 1.

TECHNIQUE: The patient fasted for 6 hours prior to the examination. Blood glucose at time of FDG injection: 112 mg/dL (acceptable; <200 mg/dL threshold met). Approximately 12.4 mCi of F-18 FDG was administered intravenously. Imaging was performed approximately 60 minutes post-injection. Low-dose CT was acquired for attenuation correction and anatomic localization from skull base to mid-thighs, followed by PET emission imaging over the same field of view. Images were reviewed in axial, coronal, and sagittal planes with fused PET/CT and standalone PET and CT displays.

FINDINGS:

Head and Neck: No FDG-avid lesions identified in the visualized portions of the head and neck. No pathologic cervical or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy.

Chest:
- Lungs: FDG-avid right upper lobe mass measuring 4.3 x 3.8 cm with SUVmax 12.4. The mass is spiculated with pleural abutment but no definite chest wall invasion. No additional pulmonary nodules or masses. No FDG-avid lesions in the left lung. No pleural effusion bilaterally.
- Mediastinum: FDG-avid right paratracheal lymph node (station 4R) measuring 2.1 cm in short axis with SUVmax 8.1. FDG-avid subcarinal lymph node (station 7) measuring 1.5 cm in short axis with SUVmax 6.3. No FDG-avid contralateral mediastinal or hilar lymphadenopathy.
- Heart: Normal cardiac size. No pericardial effusion.

Abdomen and Pelvis: Liver demonstrates homogeneous FDG uptake without focal lesions. Bilateral adrenal glands are normal in size and morphology without abnormal FDG uptake. Spleen, pancreas, and kidneys are unremarkable. No mesenteric or retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy. No FDG-avid pelvic masses or lymphadenopathy. Bowel demonstrates physiologic FDG uptake.

Musculoskeletal: No FDG-avid osseous lesions identified. No suspicious lytic or blastic skeletal abnormalities on low-dose CT.

IMPRESSION:

1. Intensely FDG-avid right upper lobe mass (4.3 x 3.8 cm, SUVmax 12.4) highly suspicious for primary lung malignancy.

2. FDG-avid ipsilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy involving station 4R (2.1 cm, SUVmax 8.1) and station 7 (1.5 cm, SUVmax 6.3), consistent with nodal metastatic disease. Findings are consistent with at least N2 nodal involvement.

3. No evidence of distant metastatic disease. No FDG-avid lesions in the contralateral lung, liver, adrenal glands, skeleton, or brain (within field of view).

4. Overall staging assessment: Metabolically active right upper lobe mass with ipsilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy, consistent with clinical stage III lung malignancy pending tissue confirmation. No distant metastases identified on PET/CT.

RECOMMENDATION: Brain MRI with contrast to complete intracranial staging (scheduled September 25, 2024). Tissue sampling of mediastinal lymph nodes recommended for pathologic confirmation of nodal involvement and molecular profiling. Results discussed with Dr. Menendez for coordination of EBUS-TBNA biopsy.
