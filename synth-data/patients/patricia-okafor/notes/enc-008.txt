ASSESSMENT:
1. Right upper lobe mass, 4.2 cm, spiculated, with ipsilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy — highly suspicious for primary lung malignancy. The spiculated morphology, mediastinal nodal involvement at stations 4R and 7, and the clinical context of persistent cough and unintentional weight loss in a patient with significant secondhand smoke exposure are collectively concerning for non-small cell lung cancer. Radiographic staging suggests at least N2 disease. Tissue diagnosis and formal staging are required.
2. Persistent cough — likely secondary to the right upper lobe mass.
3. Unintentional weight loss of 8 lbs — concerning for malignancy-related cachexia vs constitutional symptom of underlying malignancy.
4. Mild anemia (Hgb 11.8) and mild hypoalbuminemia (3.4) — may be related to underlying malignancy.
5. Essential hypertension — well controlled on current regimen.

PLAN:
1. Pulmonary function testing ordered for 08/19/2024 to establish baseline lung function prior to any potential surgical or oncologic intervention. Will obtain full spirometry, lung volumes, and DLCO.
2. Urgent referral to MD Anderson Cancer Center, Thoracic Oncology — Dr. Carlos Menendez — for staging workup and tissue diagnosis. Referral initiated today. Will include CT images and this consultation note.
3. Discussed findings in detail with patient and her husband Emeka. Explained the CT findings, including the spiculated mass and lymphadenopathy. Conveyed high clinical suspicion for lung malignancy. Discussed the need for further workup including PET/CT, possible biopsy, and molecular testing that will be coordinated by the oncology team. Patient expressed understanding and asked appropriate questions. She is anxious but wishes to proceed expeditiously with evaluation.
4. Advised patient to contact our office if she develops worsening dyspnea, hemoptysis, fever, or chest pain prior to follow-up.
