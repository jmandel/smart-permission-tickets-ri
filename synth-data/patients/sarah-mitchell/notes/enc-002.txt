DIAGNOSTIC FORMULATION:
Ms. Mitchell meets DSM-5 criteria for Bipolar II Disorder (F31.81). She has a documented history of recurrent hypomanic episodes (lasting 4-7 days, with decreased need for sleep, increased goal-directed activity, pressured speech, and impulsive spending) alternating with major depressive episodes. The current presentation represents a medication-induced hypomanic episode precipitated by sertraline, which is a well-recognized phenomenon in undiagnosed bipolar spectrum illness. Her prior diagnosis of major depressive disorder is reclassified as the depressive component of bipolar II disorder.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Bipolar II disorder (F31.81) - newly diagnosed. Current episode hypomanic, medication-induced (sertraline). Historical pattern of recurrent hypomanic and depressive episodes since college years.
2. Major depressive disorder (F32.1) - reclassified as bipolar II depressive episodes; resolved as standalone diagnosis.

PLAN:
1. MEDICATION CHANGES:
   a. Taper sertraline over 4 weeks:
      - 100 mg to 50 mg daily x 2 weeks
      - 50 mg to 25 mg daily x 2 weeks
      - Then discontinue

      - 25 mg daily x 2 weeks
      - 50 mg daily x 2 weeks
      - 100 mg daily x 2 weeks
      - 150 mg daily x 2 weeks

   c. Patient counseled extensively regarding lamotrigine rash risk. Instructed to discontinue medication and seek immediate medical attention if any rash develops, particularly with mucosal involvement, fever, or systemic symptoms.
