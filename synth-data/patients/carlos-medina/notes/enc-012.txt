TELEPHONE ENCOUNTER NOTE

Date: 11/03/2021
Patient: Carlos R. Medina   DOB: 08/14/1974   Age: 47
Provider: Dr. Alejandro Fuentes, MD, Rheumatology
Site: South Texas Rheumatology, San Antonio, TX

REASON FOR CALL: Discussion of abnormal liver function tests resulted 11/01/2021.

DISCUSSION:
I called Mr. Medina to review his November 1 laboratory results. His ALT is 98 U/L and AST is 82 U/L, both more than twice the upper limit of normal, representing a significant increase from his September 2021 values (ALT 52, AST 48). He is currently on methotrexate 20 mg weekly, which was increased from 15 mg in June 2021 for persistently active seropositive rheumatoid arthritis (M05.79). This pattern of progressive transaminase elevation on dose-escalated methotrexate is consistent with methotrexate hepatotoxicity.

Mr. Medina reports 2-3 weeks of nausea and fatigue, which he had attributed to his busy fall teaching and coaching schedule. I explained that these symptoms are likely related to the hepatotoxicity and liver enzyme elevation.

Additional hepatic workup was reviewed: total bilirubin 0.8 mg/dL (normal), albumin 3.8 g/dL, alkaline phosphatase 78 U/L (normal). These values argue against significant hepatic synthetic dysfunction or cholestatic injury. Hepatitis B surface antigen and hepatitis C antibody were ordered to exclude intercurrent viral hepatitis.

ORDERS:
1. Methotrexate 20 mg weekly — DISCONTINUED immediately due to hepatotoxicity
2. Continue folic acid 1 mg daily
3. Continue naproxen 500 mg PO PRN for joint pain
4. Hepatitis B surface antigen — ordered
5. Hepatitis C antibody — ordered
6. Patient instructed to avoid all alcohol until LFTs normalize
7. Repeat LFTs in 4-6 weeks to confirm normalization

FOLLOW-UP RESULTS (drawn 11/05/2021):
Hepatitis B surface antigen: Negative
Hepatitis C antibody: Negative
No evidence of intercurrent viral hepatitis. Transaminase elevation attributed to methotrexate hepatotoxicity.

PLAN:
Repeat comprehensive metabolic panel with LFTs in 4-6 weeks to document normalization after methotrexate discontinuation. Office visit scheduled for December 2021 to reassess RA disease activity off DMARD therapy and discuss alternative DMARD options. Mr. Medina may continue naproxen 500 mg as needed for symptom control in the interim. He verbalized understanding of the medication change and the importance of alcohol avoidance.
