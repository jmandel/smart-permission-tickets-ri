DISCHARGE SUMMARY

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Progressive shortness of breath, orthopnea, abdominal bloating, and bilateral leg swelling.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
Denise Walker is a 58-year-old woman with known HFrEF, paroxysmal atrial fibrillation, type 2 diabetes mellitus, CKD stage 3b, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia who presented after several weeks of disrupted medication access following relocation from Arizona to New Mexico. She reported worsening dyspnea, 3-pillow orthopnea, abdominal fullness, and increasing bilateral lower-extremity edema. In the ED she was hypertensive and tachycardic with irregular rhythm, consistent with acute decompensated heart failure with volume overload and atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response. She denied infectious symptoms, and CBC showed no leukocytosis.

HOSPITAL COURSE:
On presentation, ED vitals were BP 168/102 mmHg, HR 118 bpm irregular, RR 24/min, Temp 98.0 F, SpO2 91% on room air improving to 96% on 2 L nasal cannula, weight 205 lb. Admission labs were notable for BNP 1286 pg/mL, high-sensitivity troponin 19 ng/L with repeat 18 ng/L and no dynamic rise, creatinine 1.72 mg/dL, BUN 34 mg/dL, potassium 4.8 mmol/L, sodium 137 mmol/L, glucose 238 mg/dL, A1c 8.8%, and magnesium 1.8 mg/dL. Chest radiograph showed pulmonary vascular congestion with small bilateral pleural effusions. ECG demonstrated atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response at 122 bpm. Telemetry later showed rate-controlled atrial fibrillation with intermittent sinus rhythm.

She was treated for acute on chronic systolic heart failure with IV furosemide 40 mg BID, supplemental oxygen initially, strict I/O monitoring, daily weights, and electrolyte repletion. Repeat transthoracic echocardiogram showed LVEF 33%, global LV hypokinesis, mildly reduced RV function, moderate left atrial enlargement, and no LV thrombus, consistent with known HFrEF. Guideline-directed medical therapy was re-established after medication lapse, including restart of carvedilol 6.25 mg BID once perfusion stabilized, re-initiation of sacubitril/valsartan 24/26 mg BID, continuation of empagliflozin 10 mg daily, and restart of apixaban 5 mg BID for stroke prevention in atrial fibrillation. Atorvastatin 40 mg nightly was continued.

Renal function worsened transiently during diuresis with creatinine peaking at 1.89 mg/dL and eGFR nadir around 30 mL/min/1.73 m2, then improved to creatinine 1.63 mg/dL and eGFR about 36 by discharge, consistent with cardiorenal physiology during decongestion. Diabetes was managed inpatient with insulin glargine 24 units nightly plus correctional insulin. She responded appropriately to diuresis with improvement in dyspnea and edema; weight decreased from 205 lb on admission to 197 lb by discharge. Oxygen was weaned off prior to discharge.
