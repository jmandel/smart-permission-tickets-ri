SUBJECTIVE:
58-year-old woman with longstanding type 2 diabetes mellitus, CKD, hypertension, HFrEF, paroxysmal atrial fibrillation, hyperlipidemia, and diabetic peripheral neuropathy seen for worsening glycemic control, kidney disease follow-up, and burning foot pain during a period of significant caregiving stress. She reports missed meals and inconsistent medication timing while caring for her husband, with higher home sugars, worsening numbness of the toes, and nocturnal burning pain in both feet. She also reports GI upset on metformin. Grief and stress were discussed; PHQ-2 score 2/6. She denies self-harm thoughts. No foot ulcers or recent injury reported.

OBJECTIVE:
Vitals: BP 154/92 mmHg, HR 84 bpm, RR 16/min, Temp 98.4 F, SpO2 98%, wt 192 lb, ht 64 in, BMI 33.0 kg/m2.
General: Alert, oriented, in no acute distress.
Cardiopulmonary: Breathing unlabored.
Extremities/Feet: No ulcers. Callus formation at metatarsal heads bilaterally. Monofilament sensation diminished at 6/10 points bilaterally. Reduced vibration sense at great toes bilaterally.
Psych: Tearful at times when discussing caregiver burden/grief, cooperative, denies SI.
Labs: A1c 9.4%, fasting glucose 214 mg/dL, creatinine 1.56 mg/dL, eGFR approximately 38 mL/min/1.73 m2, BUN 29 mg/dL, potassium 4.7 mmol/L, bicarbonate 23 mmol/L, urine albumin/creatinine ratio 342 mg/g, LDL 92 mg/dL, hemoglobin 12.1 g/dL.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Type 2 diabetes mellitus, poorly controlled, with hyperglycemia. A1c increased to 9.4% in the setting of caregiving stress, irregular meals, and inconsistent medication timing.
2. Diabetic peripheral neuropathy, worsening. Bilateral burning foot pain and sensory loss on exam consistent with progression.
3. Diabetic kidney disease with CKD stage 3b by trend. Creatinine 1.56 with eGFR about 38 and significant albuminuria (UACR 342 mg/g). Renal reserve has worsened.
4. Hypertension, above goal today at 154/92 mmHg, concerning given CKD and diabetes.
5. Hyperlipidemia. LDL 92 mg/dL; statin therapy remains indicated.
6. Psychosocial stress/grief related to caregiving. Mood symptoms present but no self-harm thoughts.

PLAN:
1. Stop metformin due to GI intolerance and worsening renal function.
2. Increase insulin glargine from 18 units to 24 units nightly. Reviewed titration instructions to increase by 2 units every 3 nights until fasting glucose is less than 140 mg/dL.
3. Continue empagliflozin 10 mg daily for glycemic control and renal/cardiac benefit.
4. Start gabapentin 300 mg nightly for neuropathic foot pain. Reviewed sedation precautions.
5. Renew atorvastatin 40 mg nightly.
6. Diabetic foot exam completed today. Reinforced daily foot checks, skin care, and prompt evaluation for sores or infection.
7. Repeat CMP/BMP, renal function, urine albumin, and A1c in approximately 3 months.
8. Referral paperwork completed for ongoing cardiology follow-up and future nephrology review given CKD progression and comorbid HFrEF/HTN.
9. Counseled on medication adherence, regular meals, stress effects on glucose, and return precautions. Follow-up planned in about 3 months, though continuity may be affected by upcoming move.
