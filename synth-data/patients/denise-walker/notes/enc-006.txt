She reports blurred central vision in the left eye. No symptoms suggestive of retinal tear or detachment were reported. Morning glucose from patient log was 162 mg/dL. History is notable for chronic suboptimal glycemic control and hypertension, both discussed as contributing factors to current retinal findings.

OCT macula: OD central macular thickness 289 microns without center-involving edema. OS center-involving diabetic macular edema with central subfield thickness 412 microns.

Procedure: Intravitreal bevacizumab 1.25 mg/0.05 mL injected OS today by Dr. Neha Kapur after topical anesthesia and povidone-iodine prep. Procedure support provided by Teresa Lucero, RN.

3. Type 2 diabetes mellitus with chronic hyperglycemia, contributing to progression of retinal disease.
4. Hypertension, currently controlled by intake BP today, but ongoing BP control remains important for limiting retinopathy progression.

PLAN:
