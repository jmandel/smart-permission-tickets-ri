ASSESSMENT:
1. CKD stage 3b with albuminuria, slowly progressive but outpatient-stable. Renal function remains reduced with creatinine 1.74 and eGFR about 33, with persistent albuminuria (UACR 248 mg/g), consistent with ongoing diabetic kidney disease/cardiorenal risk.
2. Mild hyperkalemia risk, potassium 5.1 mmol/L. This is likely related in part to ongoing RAAS/neprilysin inhibition used for heart-failure management. At present the potassium elevation is mild and does not outweigh the renal and cardiovascular benefit of therapy.
3. Hypertension, well controlled. Office BP is 128/76 and home log is consistently at goal.
4. HFrEF on guideline-directed medical therapy. Sacubitril/valsartan provides BP and albuminuria benefit and remains clinically appropriate to continue.
5. Mild anemia of CKD/chronic disease pattern, hemoglobin 11.4 g/dL, stable for outpatient monitoring.

PLAN:
1. Continue current renal-protective and heart-failure regimen, including sacubitril/valsartan, as BP and albuminuria benefit currently outweigh mild potassium concern.
2. Counsel on moderation of high-potassium foods and maintaining consistent hydration.
3. Continue sodium restriction and strict NSAID avoidance.
4. Repeat BMP in 2 to 3 weeks to reassess potassium and renal function.
5. Place standing order for repeat renal panel and urine albumin testing prior to next nephrology follow-up.
6. Follow up with nephrology in 6 months, sooner for worsening BP control, decreased urine output, progressive edema, dyspnea, or other change in clinical status.
7. No dialysis planning indicated at this time given current outpatient stability.
