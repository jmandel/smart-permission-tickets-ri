SUBJECTIVE:
58-year-old Black woman with history of type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, HFrEF, paroxysmal atrial fibrillation, and CKD referred for new nephrology consultation for CKD stage 3b with albuminuria after recent heart-failure hospitalization. She recently relocated to Albuquerque and is re-establishing specialty care. She reports home BP readings mostly in the 120s to 130s systolic. She denies NSAID use, gross hematuria, or any new foamy urine beyond what she has noted over the years with diabetes. No interval symptoms suggesting an acute nephritic or obstructive process were reported. Recent course notable for heart-failure admission with ongoing diuretic therapy, raising concern for mixed diabetic kidney disease and cardiorenal contribution to renal dysfunction.

Relevant renal risk factors include longstanding diabetes and hypertension. Current kidney-protective/cardiac medications include sacubitril/valsartan and empagliflozin. Medication safety and nephrotoxin avoidance reviewed in detail.

OBJECTIVE:
Vitals: BP 136/84 mmHg seated, HR 72 bpm, RR 16/min, Temp 97.7 F, SpO2 98%, weight 193 lb, height 64 in, BMI 33.1 kg/m2

General: Alert, oriented, in no acute distress.
CV: Heart rate controlled.
Resp: Breathing comfortably, no respiratory distress.
Extremities: No exam findings suggesting acute volume overload documented at this visit.

Labs: Creatinine 1.61 mg/dL, eGFR 37 mL/min/1.73 m2, BUN 28 mg/dL, potassium 4.7 mmol/L, sodium 140 mmol/L, bicarbonate 23 mmol/L, phosphorus 3.9 mg/dL, albumin 3.8 g/dL, intact PTH 74 pg/mL, 25-hydroxy vitamin D 28 ng/mL, hemoglobin 11.6 g/dL, ferritin 118 ng/mL, transferrin saturation 24%, urine albumin-creatinine ratio 286 mg/g. Urine sediment bland.

ASSESSMENT:
1. CKD stage 3b with persistent albuminuria, most consistent with diabetic kidney disease and hypertensive kidney disease. Current renal function with creatinine 1.61 and eGFR 37 is stable enough for outpatient management. Bland urine sediment, absence of gross hematuria, and overall chronic course do not suggest an alternative glomerular process; no biopsy or acute nephrologic workup indicated at this time.
2. Albuminuria, UACR 286 mg/g, persistent and clinically significant. Ongoing RAAS-based therapy and SGLT2 inhibitor are appropriate for renal protection.
3. Hypertension, reasonably controlled in clinic and by home readings, with systolic pressures mostly in the 120s to 130s.
4. Cardiorenal physiology/HFrEF on diuretic therapy contributing to renal hemodynamics and requiring continued monitoring of creatinine, potassium, and volume status.
5. Mild CKD-associated mineral bone disease parameters with PTH 74 pg/mL and vitamin D insufficiency at 28 ng/mL.
6. Mild anemia of CKD/chronic disease pattern with hemoglobin 11.6 g/dL, ferritin 118 ng/mL, and transferrin saturation 24%, not requiring acute intervention.

PLAN:
1. Continue sacubitril/valsartan and empagliflozin for both renal and cardiac benefit.
2. No kidney biopsy or additional acute workup at this time given stable chronic pattern and bland urine sediment.
3. Reinforced strict nephrotoxin avoidance. Patient instructed to avoid over-the-counter NSAIDs and to notify clinicians before any contrast studies.
4. Reviewed CKD education and medication safety, including importance of BP control, diabetes control, and coordination with cardiology while on diuretic therapy.
5. Repeat BMP and urine albumin testing prior to follow-up.
6. Continue home BP log and bring readings to next visit for review.
7. Follow up with nephrology in approximately 4 months, sooner for worsening edema, decreased urine output, rising home BP, or other interval concerns.
