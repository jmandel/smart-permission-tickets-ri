SUBJECTIVE:
58-year-old woman seen in cardiology follow-up for recently established heart failure with reduced ejection fraction and paroxysmal atrial fibrillation. She reports persistent exertional dyspnea, becoming short of breath after climbing 1 flight of stairs, and intermittent palpitations. She denies syncope, chest pain, or other acute ischemic symptoms. Recent workup earlier in 2022 confirmed HFrEF and intermittent AF. Relevant history includes hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, hyperlipidemia, chronic kidney disease, and prior ACE inhibitor intolerance with cough.

OBJECTIVE:
Vitals: BP 132/80 mmHg, HR 74 bpm, irregularly irregular at intake then regular on repeat ECG, RR 18/min, Temp 97.9 F, SpO2 97% on room air, Wt 201 lb, Ht 64 in, BMI 34.5 kg/m2
General: Alert, in no acute distress.
Cardiovascular: Rhythm irregular at intake; repeat ECG with sinus rhythm. No syncope reported. Clinical symptoms consistent with symptomatic cardiomyopathy and paroxysmal atrial arrhythmia.
Respiratory: No respiratory distress at rest; exertional dyspnea present.
Extremities: No significant acute findings documented today.
Studies/Labs:
Transthoracic echocardiogram reviewed: LVEF 35%, mild LV dilation, global hypokinesis, grade II diastolic dysfunction, mild left atrial enlargement.
ECG today: sinus rhythm at 72 bpm with premature atrial complexes.
Prior ambulatory monitor: brief atrial fibrillation runs up to 140 bpm.
BNP 412 pg/mL, creatinine 1.31 mg/dL, eGFR approximately 46 mL/min/1.73 m2, potassium 4.5 mmol/L, magnesium 1.9 mg/dL, TSH 1.7 uIU/mL.

ASSESSMENT:
1. Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction, EF 35%, NYHA class II-III. Symptomatic with exertional dyspnea. Echo shows mild LV dilation, global hypokinesis, grade II diastolic dysfunction, and mild LA enlargement. Renal function and potassium acceptable for initiation of ARNI therapy.
2. Paroxysmal atrial fibrillation. Prior ambulatory monitor documented AF with rates up to 140 bpm. ECG today shows sinus rhythm with PACs. Given HF and other stroke risk factors, CHA2DS2-VASc score supports chronic anticoagulation.
3. Chronic kidney disease, stable for medication planning today with creatinine 1.31 and eGFR about 46; will need monitoring with HF regimen adjustments.
4. History of ACE inhibitor intolerance (cough), relevant to GDMT selection.

PLAN:
1. Continue carvedilol 6.25 mg PO BID.
2. Continue furosemide 20 mg PO each morning.
3. Stop losartan and start sacubitril/valsartan 24/26 mg PO BID after appropriate washout, given symptomatic HFrEF and prior ACE intolerance history.
4. Start apixaban 5 mg PO BID for stroke prevention in paroxysmal atrial fibrillation.
5. Reviewed ECG and echocardiogram findings with patient and updated medication list.
6. Reinforced sodium restriction and fluid restriction instructions, with counseling on symptom monitoring and when to seek care for worsening dyspnea, edema, bleeding, or recurrent sustained palpitations.
7. Follow up with Lisa Nguyen, PA-C, in 3-4 weeks to reassess tolerance, renal function/electrolytes as needed, and consider medication titration.
