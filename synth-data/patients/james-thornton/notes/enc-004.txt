MEDICAL DECISION MAKING:
This is a 47-year-old male presenting with post-operative surgical site infection 10 days following laparoscopic appendectomy for perforated appendicitis. He has fever, leukocytosis with left shift, elevated CRP, and purulent wound drainage consistent with a superficial/deep incisional SSI. Procalcitonin is mildly elevated at 0.8 ng/mL, suggesting localized bacterial infection rather than systemic sepsis. Lactate is normal at 1.2 mmol/L, which is reassuring against sepsis. Blood cultures were obtained to evaluate for bacteremia.

Given the history of perforated appendicitis with periappendiceal abscess, there is concern for possible deeper intra-abdominal infection in addition to the wound infection. Cross-sectional imaging will be needed during the admission to evaluate for residual or recurrent abscess. Empiric broad-spectrum IV antibiotics are indicated given the polymicrobial flora expected from a post-appendectomy wound infection.

The patient was started on IV piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g for broad gram-negative and anaerobic coverage. IV acetaminophen was administered for fever. IV normal saline bolus given for mild dehydration in the setting of fever.

This represents a readmission within 30 days of index surgery, consistent with a surgical complication requiring inpatient management.
