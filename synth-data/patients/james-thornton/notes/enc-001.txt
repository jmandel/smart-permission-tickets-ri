INDICATIONS:
Mr. Thornton is a 47-year-old male who presented to the Nebraska Medicine ED today with approximately 8 hours of progressive abdominal pain that began periumbilically and migrated to the RLQ. ED evaluation revealed fever to 100.9F, tachycardia to 102, WBC 16,800 with 82% neutrophils, and CRP 14.8 mg/dL. CT abdomen/pelvis with IV contrast demonstrated a dilated non-filling appendix with periappendiceal fat stranding, extraluminal gas, and a 2.3 cm rim-enhancing fluid collection adjacent to the cecum consistent with perforated appendicitis with localized abscess. Emergent operative intervention was recommended. Risks, benefits, and alternatives were discussed with the patient. He provided informed consent.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:
Pre-operative antibiotics were administered: piperacillin-tazobactam 3.375 g IV at 18:50, within the 60-minute pre-incision window. The patient was brought to OR 7 at 19:15. General endotracheal anesthesia was induced without complication. The patient was placed in supine position. The abdomen was prepped and draped in standard sterile fashion. A time-out was performed confirming patient identity, procedure, and surgical site.

A 12 mm incision was made at the umbilicus. A Veress needle was used to insufflate the abdomen to 15 mmHg. A 12 mm trocar was placed under direct visualization. Two additional 5 mm ports were placed under direct visualization in the suprapubic and left lower quadrant positions.

Upon entering the abdomen, the appendix was identified and found to be gangrenous with a visible perforation at the distal tip. Approximately 30 mL of purulent fluid was encountered in the right paracolic gutter and was aspirated. A periappendiceal abscess cavity measuring approximately 2.5 cm was identified adjacent to the base of the cecum.

The appendix was carefully mobilized from surrounding inflammatory adhesions. The mesoappendix was divided using a vascular endoscopic stapler. The appendiceal base was secured and transected with an endoscopic stapler. The staple line at the cecal base was inspected and found to be hemostatic and intact.

The abscess cavity was thoroughly irrigated with 500 mL warm normal saline until the effluent returned clear. A 10-French Jackson-Pratt drain was placed into the right lower quadrant through the right-sided 5 mm port site and secured to the skin.

The specimen (appendix) was placed in an endoscopic retrieval bag and removed through the umbilical port site. It was sent to surgical pathology (accession SP-2026-00412). The abdomen was desufflated. All port sites were closed. Skin was closed with subcuticular sutures. Sterile dressings were applied.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the PACU in stable condition.
