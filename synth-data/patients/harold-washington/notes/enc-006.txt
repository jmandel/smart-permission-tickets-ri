ASSESSMENT:
1. Alzheimer's disease, mild stage (G30.9) — Clinical presentation is consistent with probable Alzheimer's disease per NIA-AA criteria. The patient demonstrates insidious onset of progressive episodic memory impairment over approximately five months with corroborating informant history from his wife. Objective cognitive deficit is confirmed on MoCA (20/30) with predominant deficits in delayed recall (1/5) and visuospatial/executive function (2/5), consistent with a temporoparietal pattern. Brain MRI demonstrates bilateral hippocampal volume loss (left greater than right) supporting a neurodegenerative etiology. Reversible causes have been excluded with normal TSH, B12, RPR, HIV, and CMP. There are no features suggestive of Lewy body disease, frontotemporal dementia, or vascular dementia as the primary etiology, though mild small-vessel ischemic changes are noted.

2. Essential hypertension — well controlled on current regimen.
3. Type 2 diabetes mellitus — well controlled per PCP records.

PLAN:
1. Start donepezil 5 mg PO daily at bedtime. Titrate to 10 mg daily after 4 weeks if tolerated. Counseled patient and wife on common side effects including nausea, diarrhea, insomnia, and vivid dreams.
2. Discussed diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease in detail with Mr. Washington and his wife Dorothy. Explained the progressive nature of the disease, expected trajectory, and goals of current therapy (symptomatic improvement and slowing of decline rather than cure). Both expressed understanding.
3. Counseled on importance of maintaining structured daily routines, social engagement, physical activity, and cognitive stimulation.
4. Discussed home safety precautions: recommended supervised cooking, medication management assistance, and monitoring of driving ability.
5. Strongly recommended advance care planning including designation of healthcare power of attorney and documentation of care preferences while Mr. Washington retains capacity to participate in these decisions.
6. Continue current medications for hypertension, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia per Dr. Okafor.
7. Follow-up in 4-6 weeks for donepezil titration assessment and tolerability review.
8. Subsequent follow-up every 3-4 months for serial cognitive monitoring with MMSE and/or MoCA.
