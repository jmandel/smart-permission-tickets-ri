SUBJECTIVE:
Mr. Washington is a 78-year-old right-handed gentleman presenting for an initial comprehensive geriatric neurology evaluation, referred by Dr. Raymond Patel (First Coast Neurology, Jacksonville, FL) and Dr. Anita Desai (DeKalb Primary Care, Decatur, GA). His daughter, Margaret Washington-Clarke, accompanied him and served as primary historian. She brought Dr. Patel's referral letter and cognitive score trajectory documentation.

HPI: Mr. Washington was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease in April 2022 by Dr. Patel after presenting with progressive episodic memory impairment. MRI brain at that time showed mild generalized cortical atrophy and hippocampal volume loss without acute infarcts. He was started on donepezil 5 mg, titrated to 10 mg daily, and remained relatively stable through 2023 (MMSE 24 in March 2022, 23 in April 2023).

His wife Dorothy died of pancreatic cancer in June 2024. Following her death, his cognitive and functional decline accelerated markedly. Without Dorothy's daily structure and medication reminders, he became unable to manage independently. In September 2024 he was found wandering by a neighbor, disoriented. He also had a motor vehicle incident (backed into a mailbox). His daughter Margaret arranged relocation from Jacksonville to Decatur, GA in October 2024. His last MMSE with Dr. Patel in October 2024 was 19/30.

Margaret reports he currently cannot manage finances, cook, or be left alone for more than 2-3 hours. He requires assistance with bathing, dressing, and medication management. He does not drive. He remains continent and ambulatory.

PMH: Alzheimer's disease (diagnosed April 2022), essential hypertension (2015), type 2 diabetes mellitus (2015), hyperlipidemia (2015).

ASSESSMENT:
1. Alzheimer's disease, progressed from mild to moderate stage (G30.9, F00.9). MMSE 17/30, MoCA 13/30. The patient has demonstrated a five-point MMSE decline over 10 months, which is faster than typical progression and likely accelerated by significant psychosocial stressors including bereavement and interstate relocation. The cognitive profile — prominent deficits in delayed recall, orientation, and visuospatial/executive function with relative language preservation — is consistent with moderate-stage Alzheimer's disease. Functional impairment now spans multiple instrumental ADLs (finances, cooking, medication management) and some basic ADLs (bathing, dressing).

2. Caregiver/living situation transition. Daughter Margaret is sole caregiver. Home safety evaluation warranted given functional decline.

PLAN:
1. Discontinue donepezil 10 mg monotherapy. Start donepezil 10 mg / memantine 28 mg ER combination (Namzaric) — one capsule daily at bedtime. Rationale: patient has progressed to moderate-stage disease; addition of memantine may provide additional symptomatic benefit via NMDA receptor antagonism. Counseled Margaret extensively on expected benefits (stabilization of symptoms, not reversal of disease) and potential side effects (headache, dizziness, constipation, nausea).

2. Advance care planning discussion conducted with Mr. Washington (to the extent he could participate) and Margaret. Mr. Washington expressed a clear preference for comfort-focused care. He stated he does not want to be intubated or placed on a ventilator. Margaret confirmed these preferences are consistent with her understanding of his long-standing wishes. Advance directive preferences documented in chart. Recommended formal legal documentation update in the state of Georgia.

3. Referral placed for occupational therapy — home safety evaluation and adaptive strategies for ADL support.

4. Order repeat brain MRI to be obtained prior to next visit for comparison to 2022 imaging.

5. Follow-up in 3 months at this office.

6. Continue current medications for hypertension, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia per Dr. Desai's management.
